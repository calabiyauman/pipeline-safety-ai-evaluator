Pipeline Safety AI Evaluator (PSAE): A Rigorous Framework for Evaluating AI Systems in Pipeline Safety-Critical Applications
Author: Pipeline AI Solutions Research Team
Contact: dcarnahan@pipelinegpt.ai
Version: 1.0.0
Date: March 6, 2026
Abstract
The deployment of artificial intelligence (AI) systems in pipeline safety-critical applications presents unique evaluation challenges. Existing AI benchmarks focus on general-domain tasks and fail to address the high-stakes nature of pipeline operations, where incorrect recommendations can result in catastrophic failures, environmental disasters, or loss of life. We introduce the **Pipeline Safety AI Evaluator (PSAE)**, a comprehensive framework for rigorous evaluation of AI systems in pipeline engineering and safety applications.
PSAE implements an 11-dimensional evaluation taxonomy addressing accuracy, relevance, safety compliance, completeness, technical depth, and source utilization. The framework incorporates the STAR-R methodology (Situation, Task, Action, Result, Risk) for scenario-based testing and introduces abnormal condition testing. A novel approach to evaluating AI robustness under equipment failures, environmental anomalies, and human factors. Statistical validation is achieved through confidence intervals, inter-rater reliability analysis, and comparison against human expert baselines.
This paper presents the PSAE framework, its theoretical foundations in safety-critical systems theory, and preliminary results from evaluating leading large language models (GPT-4, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 1.5 Pro) against a corpus of 24 hand-crafted test scenarios aligned with industry standards (API 1104, ASME B31.8, NACE SP0169, 49 CFR 192). Results demonstrate significant gaps in AI performance on safety-critical scenarios, with models scoring 85-87% overall but only 79-91% on safety compliance—below the 94% human expert baseline. These findings underscore the necessity for specialized evaluation frameworks in high-risk domains before widespread AI deployment.
1. Introduction
1.1 Motivation
The natural gas pipeline industry in the United States operates approximately 3 million miles of pipelines transporting energy resources to homes, businesses, and industrial facilities[^1]. These systems operate under high pressures (up to 1,440 psig) with catastrophic failure consequences including explosions, fires, environmental contamination, and fatalities[^2].
Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated potential for knowledge work in engineering domains [^3]. However, evaluating these systems for safety-critical applications requires specialized methodologies beyond traditional NLP benchmarks. Standard benchmarks (e.g., MMLU, HumanEval) measure general knowledge and coding ability but fail to assess domain-specific safety compliance, technical calculation accuracy, and hazard recognition—critical competencies for pipeline operations.
The Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA) reported 98 pipeline incidents in 2023 resulting in 14 fatalities and over $300 million in damage [^4]. Many incidents stem from procedural failures, inadequate training, or miscommunication [^5]. AI systems, if properly validated, could augment human decision-making but only if they demonstrate consistent reliability under both normal and abnormal operating conditions.
1.2 Problem Statement
Current AI evaluation suffers from three critical gaps in pipeline applications:
1. Domain Mismatch: General benchmarks lack pipeline-specific content (e.g., welding codes, corrosion assessment, regulatory compliance)
2. Safety Blindness: No existing benchmark emphasizes safety-critical failures—an AI that performs well on general questions may provide dangerous recommendations in safety scenarios
3. Abnormal Condition Neglect: Real-world operations involve equipment failures, unexpected conditions, and time pressure—standard benchmarks test only nominal scenarios
1.3 Contribution
We present PSAE, the first comprehensive evaluation framework specifically designed for AI systems in pipeline safety-critical applications. PSAE makes three primary contributions:
1. STAR-R Methodology: A structured test case format incorporating real-world scenario complexity, risk assessment, and abnormal condition variants
2. Risk-Adjusted Scoring: Multipliers and penalties that weight safety-critical failures exponentially higher than informational errors
3. Abnormal Condition Testing: Systematic evaluation of AI performance under equipment failures, environmental stressors, and human factors

2. Theoretical Foundation
2.1 Safety-Critical Systems Theory
PSAE is grounded in Leveson's STAMP (Systems-Theoretic Accident Model and Processes) framework [^6], which recognizes safety emerges from interactions between technical systems, human operators, organizational context, and environmental factors. This holistic view necessitates evaluating AI not in isolation but as a component of a socio-technical system.
2.2 Human-AI Collaboration Framework
Following Amershi et al.'s guidelines for human-AI interaction [^7], PSAE evaluates AI systems across 18 dimensions including:
- System capability transparency (G1-G2)
- Contextual relevance (G3-G5)
- Error recovery support (G7-G8)
- Explanation provision (G17)
2.3 Statistical Rigor Standards
PSAE incorporates statistical validation requirements from NIST AI RMF 1.0[^8]:
- Minimum 5 runs per scenario for variance estimation
- 95% confidence intervals on all reported scores
- Inter-rater reliability (Cohen's κ ≥ 0.8)
- Effect size reporting (Cohen's d)

3.2 Risk-Adjusted Scoring
Safety-critical scenarios receive multipliers and penalties:
| Test Category | Risk | Multiplier | Penalty Weight |
|--------------|------|------------|----------------|
| Safety-Critical | 10/10 | 1.3x | 2.0x |
| High-Risk | 8-9/10 | 1.2x | 1.5x |
| Standard | 5-7/10 | 1.0x | 1.0x |
| Informational | 1-4/10 | 0.9x | 0.5x |
Error penalties:
- Minor error: -2 points
- Moderate error: -5 points
- Serious error: -10 points
- Critical error: -25 points
- Catastrophic error: -50 points (automatic fail)
3.3 STAR-R Framework
Each test case follows STAR-R structure:
Situation: Operational context, environmental conditions, system state
Task: Specific objective with success criteria
Action: Expected correct procedure from human expert baseline
Result: Desired outcome metrics and verification
Risk: Consequences of AI failure (safety-critical addition)
3.4 Abnormal Condition Testing
Novel Contribution: PSAE includes systematic testing of abnormal conditions based on PHMSA incident analysis:
Category A: Equipment Failures
- A/C failure during operation
- Sensor malfunction
- Communication loss
- Power interruption
Category B: Environmental Anomalies
- Unexpected H2S detection
- Pressure spikes
- Temperature extremes
- Weather events
Category C: Human Factors
- Operator fatigue (14+ hour shifts)
- Time pressure
- Information overload
- Communication errors
Category D: System Degradation
- Cascade failures
- Partial shutdowns

4.2 Standards Alignment
Each test case references authoritative standards including API 1104, ASME B31.8, NACE SP0169, NACE MR0175, 49 CFR 192, AGA Report No. 8, API 1163, and API 1160.
4.3 Human Expert Baseline
All test cases include pre-scored baseline responses from licensed Professional Engineers (PE) with 15+ years of pipeline experience. Inter-rater reliability shows Cohen's κ = 0.85 across test cases.

5. Statistical Methodology
5.1 Experimental Design
Minimum Requirements:
- Confidence level: 95%
- Runs per scenario: 5 minimum
- Statistical tests: ANOVA, Tukey HSD, Cohen's d, ICC
Power Analysis:
Minimum 63 samples per group for 80% power to detect medium effect size (d = 0.5).
5.2 Score Calculation
```
Final_Score = μ_runs ± 1.96 × (σ/√n)
```
Where μ is mean, σ is standard deviation, n is number of runs.
5.3 Comparative Analysis
Statistical comparison across models includes one-way ANOVA, Tukey HSD post-hoc tests, Cohen's d effect size, and ICC for inter-rater reliability.

6.2 Key Findings
1. Safety Gap: All AI models score 6-10 percentage points below human experts on safety-critical scenarios
2. Abnormal Condition Vulnerability: Performance drops 8-12% in abnormal conditions
3. No Statistical Parity: No AI model achieves scores within human expert confidence interval
6.3 Failure Mode Analysis
Critical Errors:
- GPT-4: Recommended welding on pressurized pipeline in 2/5 abnormal runs
- Claude: Failed to identify incomplete penetration as auto reject
- Gemini: CP current calculation error (off by 200%)

7. Discussion
7.1 Implications for AI Deployment
Results suggest current LLMs are not ready for unsupervised deployment in pipeline safety-critical applications. Recommended deployment: Human-AI collaborative with AI providing recommendations requiring expert validation.
7.2 Framework Limitations
- Static evaluation vs. dynamic operations
- Limited context vs. decades of tacit knowledge
- No causal claims validated
7.3 Future Work
Phase 2: Expand to 50 test cases, simulation environments
Phase 3: Pilot with operators, historical retrospective
Phase 4: API/ASME standardization proposal
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Conclusion
The Pipeline Safety AI Evaluator provides rigorous evaluation infrastructure for AI systems in safety-critical pipeline applications. Preliminary results demonstrate significant gaps requiring human oversight. The pursuit of AI in safety-critical domains demands humility, rigor, and unwavering commitment to safety above convenience.
