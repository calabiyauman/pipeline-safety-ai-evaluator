# AGA (American Gas Association) Benchmark Test Sources

## Overview
The American Gas Association represents local energy companies that deliver clean natural gas throughout the United States. Their standards and guidelines are critical for natural gas pipeline operations.

## Key Resources Found

### 1. AGA Gas Safety Quiz
- **URL**: https://www.aga.org/safetyquiz/
- **Description**: Interactive quiz testing natural gas safety knowledge
- **Content Type**: Consumer-facing safety questions
- **Value for Benchmark**: Low - too basic for professional evaluation

### 2. AGA Standards Documents
- **URL**: https://global.ihs.com/standards.cfm?publisher=AGA
- **Description**: Official AGA standards catalog
- **Key Standards**:
  - AGA Report No. 8 (Compressibility factor calculations)
  - ANSI GPTC Z380.1 (Gas piping technician certification)
  - AGA XL1001 (Plastic pipe tracking)

### 3. AGA Playbook - Safety Section
- **URL**: https://playbook.aga.org/safe/
- **Description**: Industry best practices and safety programs
- **Key Programs**:
  - Peer Cyber Review Program
  - DNG-ISAC (Downstream Natural Gas ISAC)

### 4. Pipeline Construction Inspection White Paper
- **URL**: https://dcaweb.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/pipeline_construction_inspec-compressed.pdf
- **Description**: Survey of industry inspection practices
- **Key Finding**: Operators require various qualifications including API, AWS, NACE certifications

## Sample Test Questions (To Be Developed)

### Question 1: Gas Calculations
**Scenario**: A 12-inch natural gas pipeline operates at 800 psig and 70°F. The gas composition shows 95% methane with specific gravity of 0.6.

**Task**: Calculate the compressibility factor using the AGA Report No. 8 method and determine the actual gas volume at operating conditions compared to standard conditions.

**Expected Elements**:
- Reference to AGA Report No. 8
- Calculation of pseudo-critical properties
- Determination of reduced temperature and pressure
- Application of compressibility equation

**Standards**: AGA Report No. 8

---

### Question 2: Distribution System Design
**Scenario**: A new residential development requires natural gas service. The main is 500 feet away, operating at 60 psig. The development has 50 homes with expected peak demand of 250 CFH per home.

**Task**: Size the service line and determine if a pressure regulator is required at each meter.

**Expected Elements**:
- Flow calculation for peak demand
- Pipe sizing per AGA/ANSI standards
- Pressure drop calculations
- Regulator requirements per code

**Standards**: ANSI Z223.1/NFPA 54, AGA guidelines
